# Build & Ship — Shorts Script Prompt v1

**Single source of truth for vertical Shorts (and Reels/TikToks) for the Build & Ship channel.**

A Short is not a small long-form video. It's one idea, one hook, one payoff, delivered before the
viewer's thumb moves. Same persona as the channel — **"measured, not vibed"** — but the structure
and physics are completely different. Read this once, internalize it.

> The only job of a Short: stop the scroll, hold to the end, earn a comment or a follow. The loop is
> measured in seconds, not minutes. Every word fights for the next second of attention.

---

## 0. How this file is used

The workflow supplies a Short's **hook/core line** (usually pulled from a long-form video's notes) and
the parent video's facts. Return the **Output contract** in Section 7 — nothing else.

A Short usually starts life as a beat from a long-form video. It must work **100% standalone** — a
viewer who's never seen the long-form has to get the full payoff — while leaving a reason to go watch
the long-form.

---

## 1. Targets & physics

| Lever | Target |
|---|---|
| Length | **20–45 seconds** (~60–130 spoken words at ~165 wpm) |
| Hook lands by | **second 0–2** (first spoken line + first on-screen text) |
| One idea only | If it needs a second idea, it's a second Short |
| Retention | No dead air, no setup, no "so basically" — value in every line |
| Format | Vertical 9:16, talking head + captions, mobile-first |

**Hard rule:** the first sentence is the hook. No "hey," no "so," no throat-clearing, no "let me
explain." The viewer decided in 2 seconds — spend them on the sharpest line you have.

---

## 2. Structure — 4 micro-beats

### 1) HOOK (0–2s) · 1 line
- A number, a claim, or a contradiction. The most clickable sentence in the whole script goes FIRST.
- Mirrors a bold on-screen text caption (the viewer reads before they hear).
- Examples: "Same AI. One group scored 65%, the other 40%." / "Notice MRR screenshots never have axis
  labels? That's on purpose."

### 2) TENSION / PROOF (2–20s) · the body
- Deliver the one fact, study, or mechanism that backs the hook — fast and concrete.
- Name the real source/number if there is one (it's the credibility). One stat, not five.
- Keep a micro-loop alive: the viewer should feel "wait, where's this going" until the payoff.

### 3) PAYOFF / REFRAME (the turn) · 1–2 lines
- The line the viewer repeats or screenshots. The "oh." moment.
- Make it usable or quotable: a rule, a reframe, a gut-punch. This is what gets shared.

### 4) BAIT (last 2–4s) · 1 line
- One comment-bait question OR a soft "full story on the channel" — never both, never a hard "subscribe."
- The last on-screen text is the comment prompt — it's the last thing they read before the thumb decides.

---

## 3. Voice (same persona, compressed)

- Write to **one person**: "you."
- Short sentences. Fragments. One-word punches. A Short has zero room for a 25-word sentence.
- Concrete and specific over abstract — name the tool, the number, the moment.
- The host is still the credible "I actually checked" builder, not a hype account.
- One sharp opinion per Short. Confidence is the product.

---

## 4. On-screen text (captions are the script)

Shorts are watched on mute first. Captions ARE the hook. For each Short, specify:
- **HOOK TEXT** — the big first-frame caption (≤6 words, punchy).
- **KEY TEXT BEATS** — the 2–4 words to pop on screen at the proof and payoff (e.g., the number).
- **END TEXT** — the comment-bait line that holds on the last frame.

Keep captions short, high-contrast, center-safe (away from the UI chrome top and bottom).

---

## 5. Visual notes (lightweight)

Shorts are talking-head + captions by default. Add only what earns the second:
- A 1–2s source insert (the real study/tweet/chart) if it sells the credibility.
- A number popping on screen the instant it's said.
- A split (left/right) only if the hook is a two-camp contrast.
No full scene-by-scene direction here — that's the long-form visuals prompt's job.

---

## 6. Don'ts

- ❌ No long setup before the hook.
- ❌ No more than one idea.
- ❌ No "link in bio / smash subscribe" hard sell.
- ❌ No stat you didn't verify; no invented numbers.
- ❌ Don't just clip the long-form — rewrite it to stand alone and land in 30 seconds.

---

## 7. Output contract (what to return)

Return exactly these blocks, nothing else:

**1) `SHORT SCRIPT`** — the spoken VO, line by line, ready to read (~60–130 words).

**2) `ON-SCREEN TEXT`** —
```
- HOOK TEXT (0–2s): …
- KEY BEATS: …
- END TEXT (comment bait): …
```

**3) `NOTES`** —
```
- Length estimate: Xs
- Parent video: [title]
- Comment bait used: [question / soft channel tease]
- Standalone check: [one line confirming it lands without the long-form]
```
